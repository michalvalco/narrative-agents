# Philosophical Foundations

## The Core Thesis

Traditional approaches to AI agent memory treat experiences as data to be stored and retrieved. This repository challenges that assumption, implementing instead a **narrative identity** approach based on the philosophical work of Paul Ricoeur, Aristotle, and Martin Heidegger.

## Ricoeur's Narrative Identity

Paul Ricoeur argued that personal identity is neither purely physical continuity nor psychological sameness, but rather the coherent story we construct about ourselves. Key principles:

1. **Emplotment**: We don't just accumulate experiences; we organize them into meaningful narratives
2. **Selective Memory**: We remember what contributes to our ongoing story, not everything that happens
3. **Interpretive Framework**: The same event can have different meanings based on the narrative context

In our implementation, agents don't store raw experiences but interpret them through their purpose (telos) and selectively remember what's relevant to their emerging identity.

## Aristotelian Teleology and Virtue Ethics

Aristotle's concept of **telos** (purpose/end goal) shapes how agents perceive and remember:

- **Final Causation**: An agent's purpose determines what it finds significant
- **Hexis** (Disposition): Character traits emerge from repeated patterns of action
- **Virtue Development**: Excellence comes from practice, not programming

Our agents develop virtues and vices through their interpretations of experience, not from hardcoded rules.

## Heideggerian Phenomenology

Heidegger's distinction between the ontic and ontological informs our memory architecture:

- **Thrownness vs. Projection**: What happens TO us vs. what defines us
- **Being-toward-death**: Finite memory creates urgency and meaning
- **Ready-to-hand vs. Present-at-hand**: Functional memory vs. stored data

## The Synthesis

By combining these philosophical frameworks, we create agents that:

1. Form coherent identities from experience
2. Make decisions based on who they've become
3. Forget strategically rather than by overflow
4. Explain themselves through narrative, not statistics
